“We learn by chess the habit of not being discouraged by present bad appearances in the state of our affairs, the habit of fighting for a favorable change, and that of persevering in the search of resources.” (Benjamin Franklin)

The Checklist

  1. Checks
  2. Captures
  3. Threaten
  4. Thwart

Opening Principles

  • The checkboard is a hill. The centre is the most important space.
  • Castling must be done early.
  • First we can move pawns but then we must defend them.
  • Try to go towards the PERFECT setup.
  • Knights love the centre.
  • Bishops love open diagonals.

Calculation Principles

  • Determine all meaningful options before going deep.
  • Consider forcing moves first.
  • Calculate maximum 3 moves ahead.
    • Calculate for both sides.
    • If a combination fails, try reordering the moves.

Endgame Principles

  • King stuck in last rank: make sure that a way to escape remains.
  • Trapped queen: Knight and Bishop can checkmate if your development is bad.
  • Scholar's mate: Thwart with Pawns and then with Knight.
  • Basic mating strategies:
    • 2 Rooks, at initial/last rank/file.
    • Queen and King, at corners.
    • Rook and King, its a long one.
    • 2 Bishops, at board end-corners.

Gameplay Styles

We can understand gameplay styles as a broad distribution across the following axes.

  • Dynamics v/s Statics
  • Intuition v/s Calculation

Gameplay styles mostly come into play during the middle game. Positional gameplay (statics) is based on playing in a way so as to utilize pawn structures, king safety, and development or activation of all pieces. Strictly positional players try to avoid unfamiliar positions, or positions in which they are being attacked.

Dyanamic gameplay involves building up an attack and fighting constantly for a significant advantage (towards a win and not "better position"). Dynamics often involves tactics. Chess tactics are almost always some form of double attack. They are the result of geometric patterns which occur again and again. After you have played awhile, you will recognize these patterns without thinking.

However, tactics are simple. The beauty of chess lies in strategizing or making long range plans (which unlike tactics you need to think about). Without it chess becomes an "empty move-by-move threat-by-threat affair".

Lastly, intuition is based on understanding of the game both positional and attacking while calculation is just that - calculation (I love the word)!

  • Tactics is seeing, strategy is thinking.
  • Think about strategy during your opponent's turn.
  • Look for tactics during your turn.
  • Attention and courage is all you need.
